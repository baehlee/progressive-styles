Eden Prairie · Twin Cities Metro

Living in Eden Prairie.

Eden Prairie sits in the southwest corner of the metro, and like its neighbors it has topped national "best places to live" rankings. The draw is a familiar trio — excellent schools, abundant parks and lakes, and a deep base of local employers — wrapped in a green, well-planned suburb along the Minnesota River bluffs.

For buyers, Eden Prairie offers newer construction across a wide range, from townhomes near the town center to executive homes overlooking the lakes and river valley. It's a place that rewards a long-term horizon.

What buyers should know.

The neighborhoods

Homes cluster around the city's lakes — Staring Lake, Round Lake, and Bryant Lake — and along the wooded bluffs above the Minnesota River. Closer to the Eden Prairie Center and the town center you'll find condos and townhomes; farther out, larger lots and newer subdivisions.

Schools & getting around

Eden Prairie Schools is a major reason families relocate here and a steady driver of demand. Highways 212, 169, and I-494 make for quick commutes, and the METRO Green Line extension brings light rail into the city — a long-term boost for both access and home values near the stations.

Parks & everyday life

With the river valley, dozens of lakes, and a large network of trails, Eden Prairie feels green in every direction. Flying Cloud fields, the Outdoor Center, and miles of paths make it easy to spend weekends outside without leaving town.

The housing stock

Expect mostly newer homes — 1980s through today — including townhomes, traditional two-stories, and higher-end homes on premium lake and bluff lots. Even with newer construction, updates and systems vary, so a careful eye on value still pays off.

Who Eden Prairie fits

It's a strong match for families chasing top schools and green space, professionals who want a short commute and coming light rail, and buyers who value a well-run, amenity-rich suburb. If you want new-ish, leafy, and convenient, start here.

On the horizon

Two things make Eden Prairie worth watching. First, the METRO Green Line extension is adding light-rail stations, and homes within an easy reach of transit tend to hold their value especially well over time. Second, the city's deep base of local employers keeps demand steady even when the broader market wobbles. For buyers, that means thinking a step ahead about where the rail and the jobs are.
