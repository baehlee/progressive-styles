Edina, Minnesota

Living in Edina.

Edina is the polished first-ring suburb just southwest of Minneapolis — tree-lined, meticulously kept, and built around two things people move here for: schools and convenience. It's where Southdale Center opened in 1956 as the country's first fully enclosed shopping mall, and where the 50th & France district still draws the whole metro for its boutiques and restaurants. You get a quieter, more spacious life than the city, with downtown Minneapolis only about fifteen minutes away.

For buyers, Edina is a long-term play: strong resale, a top-rated school district, and neighborhoods that hold their value. (It's also home turf for us — our office is on France Avenue in Edina, so this is a market we know block by block.)

What buyers should know.

The neighborhoods

The historic Country Club District is Edina's signature — one of the nation's earliest planned residential developments, full of Tudor and Colonial homes on curving, canopied streets. Morningside offers a more attainable, walkable entry point right on the Minneapolis border. Indian Hills, Parkwood Knolls, and Interlachen bring larger lots and executive homes, while the areas around 50th & France and Centennial Lakes add condos and townhomes for buyers who want low-maintenance and walkable.

Schools & getting around

Edina Public Schools is one of the big reasons families relocate here, and it shapes demand across every price tier. The city sits at the crossroads of Highways 62 and 100 with quick access to 494 and the airport, so commutes in almost any direction stay short. It's more car-oriented than the city, but the village nodes at 50th & France and Southdale put shopping and dining within easy reach.

Parks & everyday life

Centennial Lakes Park, the Braemar golf and recreation complex, and a well-funded parks system give Edina a resort-like everyday feel. The trade-off for all that polish is a higher price tier — but for many buyers the schools, the safety, and the resale stability more than justify it.

The housing stock

Expect 1920s–1940s Tudors and Colonials in the Country Club District, mid-century ramblers and splits across much of the city, tear-down-and-rebuild new construction on premium lots, and a growing supply of upscale condos and townhomes near the commercial nodes. Edina is a market where lot, location, and updates drive big price swings — exactly where a sharp eye on value pays for itself.

Who Edina fits

It's a strong match if top schools, quiet streets, and long-term resale matter more than urban walkability. Move-up families, downsizers wanting a low-maintenance condo near 50th & France, and anyone prioritizing a short, easy commute tend to thrive here. Because Edina pricing leans on schools and lot premiums, buyers who understand exactly what they're paying for — and sellers who know how to present it — have a real edge.
