Plymouth · Twin Cities Metro

Living in Plymouth.

Plymouth is one of Minnesota's largest suburbs, and it has spent years near the top of "best places to live" lists for a simple reason: it nails the suburban basics. Top schools, an exceptional park system, lakes you can actually use, and an easy reach to the rest of the metro — Plymouth is built for families who want room to breathe without giving up convenience.

For buyers, that means a deep supply of newer single-family homes and townhomes, much of it built from the 1980s onward, with established trees and well-kept neighborhoods. It's a place people move to and stay.

What buyers should know.

The neighborhoods

Plymouth spreads across distinct pockets, from the lakeside neighborhoods near Medicine Lake — one of the largest lakes in Hennepin County — to newer subdivisions on the city's western edge. You'll find everything from approachable townhome communities to executive homes, with quiet cul-de-sacs and walking trails woven throughout.

Schools & getting around

Much of Plymouth feeds into the highly regarded Wayzata school district, with parts served by Robbinsdale and Osseo — so school boundaries are worth confirming address by address. Highways 55, 169, I-494 and I-394 put downtown Minneapolis, the airport, and the western suburbs all within easy reach.

Parks & everyday life

This is where Plymouth shines. French Regional Park, the Hilde Performance Center's free summer concerts, and miles of trails make the outdoors part of daily life. It's the kind of place where kids bike to the pool and neighbors actually know each other.

The housing stock

Plymouth skews newer than the first-ring suburbs: lots of 1980s–2000s construction, plus ongoing new builds on the western edge and a healthy supply of townhomes for first-time buyers and downsizers. Newer doesn't mean trouble-free, though — we'll still help you read the bones of any home before you commit.

Who Plymouth fits

It's a strong match for families who want newer homes, top schools, and serious park access, plus professionals who want a manageable commute in several directions. If your priority list starts with schools and green space, Plymouth belongs on your tour.
