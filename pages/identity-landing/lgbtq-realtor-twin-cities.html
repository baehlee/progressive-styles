What "affirming" actually means with us

It's easy to put a rainbow on a website. Here's what an affirming agent looks like in practice:

  • No assumptions. We don't guess who's on the loan, whose name goes on title, or what your family looks like. We ask, we listen, and we plan around your actual life.
  • No steering. Fair housing isn't a suggestion. We'll never quietly push you toward — or away from — a neighborhood based on who you are. You see every option.
  • Title & financing done right. For unmarried partners buying together, how you hold title matters. We'll connect you with lenders and closers who get it and structure things to protect you both.
  • Local knowledge that helps. We know which Twin Cities neighborhoods, schools, and communities our LGBTQ+ clients tend to love — and we'll share it honestly when you ask.
  • Discretion and respect. Your business is yours. We move at your pace and keep your information where it belongs.

Relocating to the Twin Cities? You picked well.

If you're moving to Minneapolis or St. Paul from out of state, here's some good news: Minnesota has been ahead of the curve for a long time. The state's Human Rights Act has protected against discrimination based on sexual orientation since 1993 — among the first in the nation — and Minnesota legalized marriage equality in 2013, before the Supreme Court did.

The Twin Cities back that up culturally. Minneapolis consistently earns a perfect score on the Human Rights Campaign's Municipal Equality Index, and Twin Cities Pride at Loring Park is one of the largest Pride celebrations in the country. Loring Park has long been the historic heart of the local LGBTQ+ community, but you'll find welcoming, vibrant neighborhoods all over the metro — from Northeast Minneapolis to Uptown to the leafy streets of St. Paul.

Questions we hear a lot

Do I really need an LGBTQ+ realtor?

You don't need one — but plenty of our clients say it's a relief to work with someone they don't have to come out to, explain their family to, or quietly size up first. Buying or selling a home is high-stakes and personal, and your agent is your advocate through all of it. Working with a team that already gets it means you spend your energy on the home, not on managing the relationship.

Are the Twin Cities a good place for LGBTQ+ families?

Yes — and not just on paper. Minnesota's legal protections are among the oldest in the country, Minneapolis scores a perfect 100 on the Human Rights Campaign's Municipal Equality Index, and there are welcoming, family-friendly neighborhoods across the metro with strong schools and real community. When you're weighing specific areas, we'll give you an honest read on the trade-offs.

Can you help same-sex or unmarried partners with financing and title?

Absolutely. When two people buy together without being married, how you hold title — joint tenancy versus tenants in common — and how the loan is structured can have real consequences later. We'll connect you with lenders and title professionals who handle this well, and make sure you both understand your options before you sign.

Do you work with transgender and nonbinary clients?

Of course. We use the name and pronouns you give us, full stop, and we handle any documentation or paperwork questions with care and zero drama.

I'm relocating from out of state — can we start before I arrive?

Yes. Many of our relocation clients never set foot in Minnesota until closing week. We run video tours, send neighborhood breakdowns, and give you a candid read on where you'll feel at home — so your first day in the Twin Cities already feels like yours.

Which Twin Cities neighborhoods do LGBTQ+ buyers tend to love?

It depends on what you're after. Loring Park and downtown Minneapolis put you in the historic center of the community and steps from Pride. Northeast Minneapolis draws the arts-and-breweries crowd; Uptown and the lakes neighborhoods balance walkability with green space; and St. Paul's Mac-Groveland, Highland Park, and Como areas are favorites for families who want character homes and good schools. Out in the suburbs, places like St. Louis Park, Richfield, and Maplewood are welcoming and more affordable. We'll match the search to your budget, commute, and the life you actually want — not a stereotype.
