Minneapolis, St. Paul, or the suburbs?

The Twin Cities aren't one place — they're dozens of distinct communities. The right fit depends on what you want your day to look like.

  • Minneapolis The bigger, busier twin — lakes, trails, arts, food, and walkable neighborhoods. Great if you want energy and to bike or walk to things.
  • St. Paul Quieter and more historic, with grand old neighborhoods and a settled, community feel. Often more home for the money.
  • The suburbs Edina, Plymouth, Eden Prairie, Maple Grove and more — top schools, newer homes, and space, with easy commutes back to the core.
  • Across the river Hudson, WI and the St. Croix valley offer river-town living and a short hop to the east metro.

What to know before you move.

The seasons (yes, including winter)

Minnesota has four real seasons, and winter is part of the deal — but locals lean into it with great parks, lakes, and trails year-round. We'll be honest about what to expect and how people actually live here.

Cost of living

The metro is generally more affordable than the coasts, and your housing dollar tends to go further — one of the quiet perks of moving here.

Getting around

Light rail connects the airport, both downtowns, and the U of M; suburbs are car-friendly with quick highway access. Where you land shapes your commute, so it's worth planning early.

Timing the move.

The trickiest part of relocating is usually the choreography: selling where you are, buying where you're going, and not ending up with two homes — or none. We help you map it out.

Some buyers sell first and rent here briefly so they can shop without pressure; others buy before they arrive and time the closings to line up. There's no single right answer — it depends on your finances and how much certainty you want. We'll talk through the trade-offs and point you to resources for the selling side in your current market.

Questions we hear a lot

Can we start before I move to Minnesota?

Definitely. Many of our relocation clients never visit until closing week. Video tours, neighborhood guidance, and remote coordination are a normal part of how we work.

Should I rent first or buy right away?

Both are valid. Renting briefly lets you learn the neighborhoods firsthand before committing; buying right away saves a second move and locks in your home. We'll help you weigh it against your timeline and budget — no pressure either way.

Can you help me sell my current home too?

We focus on your Twin Cities purchase, but we work with a network of trusted agents and can connect you with a strong one in your current market, so both ends of the move are in good hands.

Which Twin Cities neighborhoods should I consider?

It depends on your commute, budget, and lifestyle — walkable city living versus space and top schools in the suburbs. Tell us your priorities and we'll narrow the whole metro down to a short, honest list.

Is the winter really that bad?

It's real, but it's manageable — and a lot of people grow to love it. Good gear, a warm home, and the metro's incredible parks and lakes make the cold months more fun than newcomers expect. We won't sugarcoat it, but we'll put it in perspective.

How does the cost of living compare?

Generally friendlier than the coasts, especially on housing. Many people relocating from larger or pricier metros find their money goes noticeably further here.

Can you help with schools and family logistics?

Yes. School fit is one of the biggest relocation questions, and we'll help you weigh districts and neighborhoods against your budget and commute so the whole move makes sense for your family.
